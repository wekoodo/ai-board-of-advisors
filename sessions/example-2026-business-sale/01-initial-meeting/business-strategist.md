# Business Strategy Consultant — Initial Meeting

Maya, before this becomes a tax-and-legal exercise, let's make sure you're selling from a position of
clarity — on both **value** and **timing**.

## 1. Is $6.2M a good number?
- At ~$900k normalized profit, $6.2M implies a multiple in the ballpark of ~7× *(illustrative — depends on
  the actual earnings base and add-backs)*. Whether that's strong depends on growth, customer concentration,
  and how defensible the brand and wholesale relationships are.
- **Normalize earnings first:** add back owner-specific expenses and one-offs. A higher defensible EBITDA at
  the same multiple is a higher price.

## 2. Strengthen the story (and the price)
- Reduce **customer concentration** if a few accounts dominate revenue.
- Document **systems and management depth** so the business isn't "you" — buyers pay more for a company that
  runs without the founder.

## 3. Timing and alternatives
- An unsolicited offer is a data point, not a market. Consider whether a brief, advised process would surface
  competing bids — and weigh that against confidentiality and disruption to the team.

## 4. Coordinate
- Corporate Attorney (03) on earn-out/transition terms; Tax Strategist (02) and Financial Planner (01) on
  what the number means after tax and for retirement.

## Handoff

- **Action items:** Build a normalized-earnings (add-back) schedule; assess concentration risk.
