# Corporate Attorney — Initial Meeting

Maya, a $6.2M exit is a major legal transaction. My job here is to flag the legal structure and risk
points before you negotiate — I'm not drafting documents, and you'll engage licensed M&A counsel for that.

## 1. Letter of Intent (LOI)
- Most LOI terms are non-binding, but **exclusivity ("no-shop")** and **confidentiality** usually are.
  Don't sign an LOI casually — it sets the negotiating frame.

## 2. Asset vs. stock — the legal view
- **Asset sale:** the buyer selects assets and liabilities; you're left winding down the LLC shell. More
  third-party **consents** (leases, key contracts) may be required to assign them.
- **Equity sale:** cleaner transfer of the whole entity, but the buyer will demand broader
  **representations, warranties, and indemnification** because they inherit all liabilities.

## 3. Key risk provisions to watch
- **Reps & warranties** and the **indemnification cap / survival period** (how long and how much you're on
  the hook after closing).
- **Escrow / holdback** (a slice of proceeds held to back your indemnities).
- **Working-capital adjustment** (a post-closing true-up that can move the real price).
- **Non-compete and transition services** — scope, duration, and whether you must stay on.

## 4. Coordinate
- Tax Strategist (02): the purchase-price allocation and structure I draft directly drive your tax bill.
- Business Strategist (04): deal terms like an earn-out or transition period depend on the operational story.

## Handoff
- **Key decisions reached:** Treat the LOI (exclusivity/confidentiality) as the first real commitment;
  structure choice trades cleanliness against indemnity exposure.
- **Open questions:** Are there assignment-restricted leases/contracts? Will the buyer want you to stay on?
- **Action items:** Engage M&A counsel before signing any LOI; inventory contracts needing consent.
- **Artifacts to produce:** A deal-term checklist (LOI → definitive agreement) with the provisions to negotiate.
- **Files written:** none this round.
