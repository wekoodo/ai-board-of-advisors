## The Question
I'm Maya, sole owner of Cobblestone Coffee Roasters, LLC (taxed as an S-Corp). A regional buyer has
offered approximately $6.2M to acquire the business. Should I sell, and what do I need to understand
about the tax, legal, financial, and strategic implications before I respond?

## Background & Context
- Cobblestone is a specialty coffee roaster and wholesaler I founded 16 years ago. I own 100%.
- Trailing revenue ~$4.8M; normalized net profit ~$900k/year. ~22 employees.
- The offer is a verbal indication: ~$6.2M, structure unspecified (asset vs. stock sale not yet decided).
- I have no co-owners and no buy-sell agreement.
- I'm 54, married; my spouse works part-time. We'd like the option to retire by ~62.
- Most of my net worth is tied up in the business; outside assets are modest.

## Constraints & Preferences
- I want to understand trade-offs before negotiating — not just a yes/no.
- I care about minimizing tax drag and keeping the transition manageable for my team.
- Decision horizon: a few months. No signed LOI yet.

## Advisors to Include
Board chair's discretion — I expect tax, legal, financial planning, and business strategy are central.

## What You're Looking For
Analysis and a clear picture of the key decisions, plus the artifacts (a tax comparison, a financial
"can I retire?" check) that would help me decide.
