# Session Brief

## The Question
We're Priya and Marcus. We have about $62,000 saved and we're considering buying our first
single-family rental property in our city. What should we evaluate before we do, can we actually
afford it, and is this a sound move for us right now?

---

## Background & Context
- We're married, both 35, no children yet (planning within a few years).
- Combined income ~$145k: Priya is a registered nurse (~$85k), Marcus is a high-school teacher (~$60k).
  Both W-2, stable employment.
- We rent our own home (~$1,950/month) and own no real estate.
- Savings: ~$62,000 total — of which we consider ~$15,000 an existing emergency fund we'd like to keep
  intact. The remaining ~$47,000 is what we'd consider putting toward a property.
- Retirement: each of us contributes to an employer plan (~$70k combined balances); no other investments.
- Debt: ~$18,000 in student loans (moderate fixed rate); no credit-card debt; one car loan (~$9,000).
- The local market: modest single-family homes in decent rental areas run ~$240,000; comparable homes
  rent for ~$1,900/month. We have no landlord experience.

---

## Constraints & Preferences
- We want to understand what we're getting into before committing — show us how to evaluate a deal and
  whether our cash position really supports this.
- We're cautious. We don't want to drain our safety net or overextend.
- Decision horizon: the next 6–12 months. Nothing is under contract.

---

## Advisors to Include
Board chair's discretion. We expect real estate and financial planning are central.

---

## What You're Looking For
A clear picture of the key decisions and risks, a way to evaluate whether a given property is a good
deal, and an honest read on whether our finances support a first rental now — plus any artifacts (a
property evaluation checklist, an affordability/reserves check) that would help us decide.
