## STAGE 1 — Location & Neighborhood (qualify the market first)

You can fix a house. You cannot fix a location. Do this before underwriting a specific property.

| ☐ | Item | What "good" looks like / why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| ☐ | **Signed-lease rent comps** | Pull *actual signed* leases (via a local PM or agent), not asking rents or your hopes. This single number drives the entire underwriting. |
| ☐ | **Rental vacancy trend** | Stable or falling vacancy; rising vacancy means rent pressure and longer turnovers. |
| ☐ | **Days-on-market for rentals** | Short DOM = strong demand. Long DOM = you'll carry vacancy. |
| ☐ | **Employer base / job stability** | Multiple stable employers (hospitals near Priya's network, schools, government) = reliable tenant demand. Avoid single-employer towns. |
| ☐ | **School ratings** | Strong school zones support both rent and tenant quality/tenure, even for non-family tenants (resale + demand). |
| ☐ | **Crime trend** | Falling or low; check actual local crime maps, not reputation. |
| ☐ | **Pride-of-ownership signals** | Maintained yards, owner-occupant ratio, infrastructure upkeep — signs of a stable, appreciating area. |
| ☐ | **Path of growth / new development** | New infrastructure, transit, or employers nearby support appreciation; nearby decline is a warning. |
| ☐ | **Tenant-pool fit** | Does the area's typical renter match a single-family home (families, relocators) vs. apartment-dwellers? |
| ☐ | **Insurance / hazard exposure** | Flood, wildfire, hail, coastal — these swing premiums and insurability. Confirm before you're emotionally committed (tag Insurance Specialist, 08). |

**Stage 1 gate:** If the *signed-lease* rent comps don't support at least a borderline 1% ratio
at realistic prices, this market won't cash-flow a leveraged SFR. Reconsider the market before
touring houses.

## STAGE 2 — The Property (during inspection / contingency period)

Hire a licensed inspector. Do not skip this to win a bid — it is the cheapest insurance you'll buy.

### 2A. Structure & Big-Ticket Systems (the CapEx that wrecks first-timers)

| ☐ | Item | Watch for / question to answer |
|---|---|---|
| ☐ | **Roof** | Age and remaining life; a near-end roof is a $10k–$20k *(illustrative — verify locally)* line item — negotiate it. |
| ☐ | **HVAC** | Age, type, condition; replacement is major CapEx. |
| ☐ | **Foundation / structure** | Cracks, settling, water intrusion, grading. The most expensive problem to fix — walk if serious. |
| ☐ | **Plumbing** | Material (avoid known-problem types), water pressure, leaks, sewer line scope (root intrusion). |
| ☐ | **Electrical** | Panel age/capacity, knob-and-tube or aluminum wiring, GFCI/code issues. |
| ☐ | **Water heater** | Age and condition; common early replacement. |
| ☐ | **Windows / insulation / envelope** | Affects tenant utility costs and turnover appeal. |

### 2B. Environmental & Hazard

| ☐ | Item | Why |
|---|---|---|
| ☐ | **Flood-zone status** | Determines flood-insurance requirement and cost (08). |
| ☐ | **Age-related hazards** (lead paint, asbestos, etc. in older homes) | Disclosure obligations and remediation cost; landlord liability. |
| ☐ | **Radon / soil / other local hazards** | Per local norms; cheap to test, expensive to ignore. |
| ☐ | **Pest / termite / mold** | Inspection-dependent; can be a structural and health issue. |

### 2C. Title, Permits & Legal Condition

| ☐ | Item | Why |
|---|---|---|
| ☐ | **Title search / title insurance** | Liens, encumbrances, easements, boundary issues. |
| ☐ | **Permit history on additions** | Unpermitted work = insurance, resale, and code-enforcement risk. |
| ☐ | **Survey / boundaries / encroachments** | Confirm what you're actually buying. |
| ☐ | **HOA, if any** | Dues, rental restrictions/caps, special assessments — some HOAs ban or limit rentals. |
| ☐ | **Easements / shared access** | Driveways, utilities, right-of-way. |

## STAGE 3 — The Numbers & Regulatory Reality (verify before removing contingencies)

These convert a "nice house" into a "good or bad deal." Feed every verified figure into the
**deal-screen + cash-flow worksheet** (the companion artifact).

### 3A. Verify the Real Numbers (not the seller's pro-forma)

| ☐ | Item | Why |
|---|---|---|
| ☐ | **Actual property tax — and reassessment on sale** | Taxes often jump when the property changes hands; use the *post-sale* number, not the seller's old bill. Materially swings cash flow. |
| ☐ | **Real landlord-insurance quote (DP-3 + liability)** | Get an actual quote, not a guess — varies by hazard exposure. Tag Insurance Specialist (08); ask about umbrella liability. |
| ☐ | **Confirmed market rent** | From a local property manager / signed comps, not the listing's optimistic figure. |
| ☐ | **Financing terms locked** | Investment-property rate *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*, down-payment requirement (typically 20–25%), and closing costs (2–4%). |
| ☐ | **Utilities & who pays** | Landlord-paid water/trash/lawn/snow? Add to operating expenses. |
| ☐ | **Realistic rehab / make-ready budget** | Cost to get it rent-ready and any deferred maintenance from Stage 2. |

### 3B. Regulatory & Operating

| ☐ | Item | Why |
|---|---|---|
| ☐ | **Local landlord-tenant law** | Know your rights and obligations before you're a landlord. |
| ☐ | **Eviction timeline & difficulty** | A months-long eviction can erase a year of returns; tenant-friendly jurisdictions raise risk. |
| ☐ | **Rent control / rent-stabilization** | Caps on increases change the long-term model. |
| ☐ | **Rental licensing / registration / inspection rules** | Many cities require a license and periodic inspection. |
| ☐ | **Short-term-rental restrictions** | Only if an STR strategy is on the table. |
| ☐ | **Entity / ownership structure** | LLC vs. personal name — liability and lending trade-offs. Tag Corporate Attorney (03). |

## STAGE 4 — Landlord-Readiness (be honest before you commit)

| ☐ | Item | Reality check |
|---|---|---|
| ☐ | **Self-manage vs. hire (~8–10% of rent)** | Self-managing saves money but costs time and emotional bandwidth — tenant screening, 11pm maintenance calls, turnovers. Budget for management even if you DIY at first. |
| ☐ | **Operating-reserve cash beyond cash-to-close** | A separate cushion for vacancy/repairs so a turnover doesn't touch your emergency fund. Sizing is the Financial Planner's (01) call. |
| ☐ | **Tenant-screening process** | Credit, income, references, prior-landlord checks — within fair-housing rules. |
| ☐ | **Liquidity acceptance** | This is a 5–10+ year hold; selling takes months and ~6–9% in costs. Not recallable cash. |
| ☐ | **Concentration awareness** | One property, one local market = undiversified. One bad tenant or roof can wipe out a year. |
