## Decision

- **Target: 4 months of *essential* expenses ≈ $12,800** (4 × $3,200) — sized on essentials, not take-home
  pay. The single-income reality keeps Daniel off the 3-month floor; no dependents, a stable job, and no
  high-interest debt keep him off 6.
- **Hold it in a dedicated high-yield savings account (HYSA)** at an FDIC-insured institution, separate
  from everyday checking — safe, accessible in 1–2 days, real yield, zero maintenance.
- **Stop at the target.** Once funded, redirect the surplus. The asymmetry: being under-prepared can cost
  thousands now (credit-card debt or an early 401(k) withdrawal); being over-cushioned only costs slower
  growth on the excess.

## Build plan

1. Open a dedicated FDIC-insured HYSA, named "Emergency Fund."
2. Move $5,000 of the $6,000 in now; keep ~$1,000 in checking as a buffer (starting balance: $5,000 of
   $12,800).
3. Automate ~$650/month from payday → ~12 months to target (≈ $900/month → ~8–9 months).
4. Stop the transfer at ~$12,800.
5. Redeploy the freed ~$1,100/month per the priority stack: keep the full 401(k) match (already doing it),
   then a Roth IRA *(contribution limit is year-specific — illustrative; verify current-year value)*, then
   the student loan if its rate is high relative to expected after-tax returns.
