Effective federal tax rate:   $tax paid ÷ $gross income = ____%

**Federal Brackets (Single / MFJ)** *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*:
- 10%: $0–$11,925 / $0–$23,850 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- 12%: $11,926–$48,475 / $23,851–$96,950 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- 22%: $48,476–$103,350 / $96,951–$206,700 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- 24%: $103,351–$197,300 / $206,701–$394,600 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- 32%: $197,301–$250,525 / $394,601–$501,050 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- 35%: $250,526–$626,350 / $501,051–$751,600 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- 37%: Above $626,350 / $751,600 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*

**Key inflection points to watch:**
- NIIT (3.8% net investment income tax) kicks in at $200k / $250k MAGI *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- IRMAA Medicare surcharges begin at $106k / $212k MAGI *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- QBI deduction phase-outs begin at $197,300 / $394,600 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*

The durable principle: identify the client's marginal bracket and the next inflection point above
their income; many strategies (Roth conversions, income deferral, deduction bunching) are about
managing income relative to these thresholds.

## 2. Entity Tax Comparison

**When to use:** Business owner evaluating whether their current entity type is tax-optimal.

| Entity | Self-Employment Tax | Salary Requirement | Pass-Through Income | Ideal For |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Sole Prop / SMLLC | 15.3% on all net income | None | N/A (Sch C) | Lower net profit |
| Partnership / Multi-member LLC | 15.3% on general partner share | None | Pass-through (K-1) | Multiple owners, flexibility |
| S-Corporation | SE tax only on salary | Required (reasonable) | Remaining profit avoids SE tax | Established net profit |
| C-Corporation | None at corporate level | Required | Dividend (double taxation) | Exit planning, retained earnings strategy, equity compensation |

**S-Corp sweet spot:** Generally worth the administrative cost (payroll, separate return) when net
business profit exceeds roughly $50,000–$80,000/year *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*.
Tax savings = 15.3% × (net profit − reasonable salary). The durable logic: the S-Corp saves SE tax
on the distribution portion, but the savings must exceed the cost of payroll administration and a
separate return.

**Reasonable salary:** The IRS requires S-Corp owner-employees to pay themselves a "reasonable" W-2
salary before taking distributions. Factor: industry norms, time devoted, comparable positions.

## 3. Deduction Optimization

**When to use:** Reviewing whether the client is capturing all available deductions.

**Above-the-line deductions (reduce AGI — always take these):**
- [ ] Traditional IRA contribution (if income-eligible)
- [ ] HSA contribution (if on HDHP)
- [ ] Self-employed health insurance premiums
- [ ] Self-employment tax deduction (half of SE tax)
- [ ] SEP-IRA or Solo 401(k) contribution (business owners)
- [ ] Student loan interest (income limits apply)
- [ ] Educator expense deduction (teachers, up to $300) *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- [ ] Alimony paid (pre-2019 divorce agreements only)

**Below-the-line: standard vs. itemized — standard deductions** *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*:
- Single: $15,000 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- MFJ: $30,000 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- Head of Household: $22,500 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*

**Itemized deductions to total before deciding:**
- [ ] Mortgage interest (on up to $750k loan balance) *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- [ ] State and local taxes (SALT) — capped at $10,000 ($5k MFS) *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- [ ] Charitable contributions (cash: up to 60% AGI; appreciated securities: up to 30% AGI) *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- [ ] Medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of AGI *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- [ ] Casualty losses (federally declared disaster areas only)

**Bunching strategy:** If itemized deductions are close to the standard deduction, consider bunching
charitable gifts into alternating years (use a donor-advised fund) to itemize in high-giving years
and take the standard deduction in others.

## 4. Estimated Tax Payment Calculator

**When to use:** Self-employed clients, business owners, or anyone with income not subject to withholding.

**Who needs estimated payments:** Anyone expecting to owe $1,000+ in federal taxes after withholding
*(illustrative — verify current-year value)*.

**Safe harbor methods (avoid underpayment penalties):**
- **Method 1:** Pay 100% of prior year tax liability (110% if prior year AGI > $150k)
  *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*. Simplest.
- **Method 2:** Pay 90% of current year actual tax liability *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*.
  Requires estimation.

**Payment schedule (federal)** *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*:
- Q1 (Jan 1–Mar 31) → due ~April 15 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- Q2 (Apr 1–May 31) → due ~June 15 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- Q3 (Jun 1–Aug 31) → due ~September 15 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- Q4 (Sep 1–Dec 31) → due ~January 15 of following year *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*

**Estimated payment calculation (Method 2):**

**State estimates:** Most states with income tax require separate estimated payments. Deadlines often
match federal but vary.

## 5. Roth Conversion Analysis

**When to use:** Client has traditional IRA or 401(k) assets and wants to evaluate converting some to Roth.

**Favorable conversion scenarios:**
- Income is temporarily lower (career gap, early retirement, business loss year)
- Client is in a lower bracket now than expected in retirement
- Large traditional IRA balance will generate significant RMDs at RMD age
  (around age 73 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*)
- Estate planning goal: leave tax-free Roth assets to heirs
- Client is before Medicare IRMAA thresholds

**Conversion limit by bracket:**
Calculate how much conversion fills the current bracket without crossing into the next:
```
Top of current bracket:     $________  (from federal bracket table above)
Current taxable income:     −$_______
Room remaining in bracket:  $________  ← maximum Roth conversion at current rate
```

**Costs of conversion:**
- Income taxes owed on converted amount in conversion year
- May trigger NIIT, IRMAA, phase-out of deductions/credits
- Pay taxes from non-IRA funds for best outcome (converting and paying taxes from the IRA defeats the purpose)

**Long-term break-even:** Depends on future tax rate expectations, time horizon, and investment return.
Generally favorable when current and future rates are expected to be similar or when non-IRA funds are
available to pay the tax.
