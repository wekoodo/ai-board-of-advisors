## 1. Asset Allocation by Risk Profile

**When to use:** Determining or reviewing a client's target portfolio allocation.

| Risk Profile | Stocks | Bonds | Real Assets / Alternatives | Cash |
|-------------|--------|-------|---------------------------|------|
| Conservative | 30–40% | 50–60% | 5–10% | 5–10% |
| Moderate-Conservative | 45–55% | 35–45% | 5–10% | 3–5% |
| Moderate | 55–65% | 25–35% | 5–10% | 2–5% |
| Moderate-Aggressive | 70–80% | 15–25% | 5–10% | 1–3% |
| Aggressive | 80–90% | 5–15% | 5–10% | 0–2% |

**Within stocks, suggested sub-allocation:**
- US Large Cap: 40–50% of stock allocation
- US Small/Mid Cap: 15–25%
- International Developed: 20–30%
- Emerging Markets: 5–15%

**Time horizon as a modifier:**
- Under 5 years to need: shift toward conservative (capital preservation)
- 5–10 years: moderate range
- 10+ years: can tolerate aggressive allocation (time to recover from downturns)

## 2. Asset Location Optimization

**When to use:** Client has both tax-advantaged and taxable accounts and wants to minimize tax drag.

**Principle:** Place tax-inefficient assets in tax-advantaged accounts, tax-efficient assets in taxable accounts.

**Tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA, Roth IRA):**
- Bonds and bond funds (interest is taxed as ordinary income)
- REITs (high dividend distributions taxed as ordinary income)
- High-turnover actively managed funds (frequent capital gains distributions)
- Target-date funds (hold bonds; better in tax-advantaged)

**Taxable accounts (brokerage):**
- Broad US stock index funds (low turnover, qualified dividends)
- Broad international index funds (eligible for foreign tax credit)
- Tax-managed funds
- Municipal bonds (if in high tax bracket — interest is federally tax-exempt)
- Individual stocks held long-term

**Roth IRA specifically:**
- Best location for highest-growth assets (gains are tax-free forever)
- Small-cap growth, emerging markets — highest expected long-term return

## 3. Rebalancing Framework

**When to use:** Determining whether and how to rebalance a portfolio back to target allocation.

**Trigger methods:**

**Threshold-based (recommended):**
- Rebalance when any asset class drifts more than 5 percentage points from target
- Example: target 60% stocks; trigger rebalance when stocks reach 65%+ or 55%−
- Avoids excessive trading while catching meaningful drift

**Calendar-based:**
- Rebalance annually on a set date regardless of drift
- Simpler to execute; may trade unnecessarily or miss significant drift

**Hybrid:** Combine — review annually; rebalance only if threshold is breached.

**Tax-efficient rebalancing techniques:**
1. **New cash:** Direct new contributions (401k, IRA, brokerage deposits) to underweight assets — no taxes triggered
2. **Dividend reinvestment:** Reinvest distributions into underweight assets
3. **Tax-loss harvesting:** Sell overweight losing positions to realize losses; use proceeds to buy underweight assets
4. **Roth conversions:** Rebalance inside tax-advantaged accounts without tax consequences
5. **Only sell in taxable when necessary:** Prioritize above methods; selling appreciated assets in taxable accounts triggers capital gains

## 4. Diversification and Concentration Risk Checklist

**When to use:** Assessing whether a portfolio is adequately diversified or has dangerous concentration.

**Red flags — concentration risk:**
- [ ] More than 10% of total portfolio in a single stock
- [ ] More than 25% in a single sector (e.g., tech)
- [ ] More than 30% in real estate (including primary residence equity and REITs)
- [ ] More than 70% in a single country (even the US)
- [ ] More than 50% in employer stock (especially in 401k)
- [ ] No international exposure (home country bias)
- [ ] No bond or fixed income exposure (for clients within 10 years of retirement)

**Adequate diversification indicators:**
- [ ] Multiple asset classes represented (stocks, bonds, real estate/REITs, alternatives)
- [ ] Multiple geographies (US, international developed, some emerging markets)
- [ ] Multiple market caps (large, mid, small)
- [ ] Multiple sectors represented within equity allocation
- [ ] Bond duration diversification (short, intermediate, possibly long)

## 5. Annual Portfolio Review Checklist

**When to use:** Conducting a structured annual portfolio review.

**Account and holdings:**
- [ ] All accounts inventoried (401k, IRA, Roth, HSA, taxable brokerage, 529)
- [ ] Total investable assets calculated
- [ ] Current allocation percentage across all accounts calculated

**Alignment check:**
- [ ] Current allocation vs. target allocation — is rebalancing needed?
- [ ] Risk profile still appropriate for client's situation? (Major life event may shift it)
- [ ] Time horizon review — have goals or timelines changed?

**Tax review:**
- [ ] Tax-loss harvesting opportunities identified in taxable accounts
- [ ] Asset location still optimized across account types?
- [ ] Roth conversion opportunity in current tax year?

**Beneficiary and titling:**
- [ ] Beneficiary designations on all accounts reviewed and up-to-date
- [ ] Account titling reviewed (individual, joint, TOD, trust-owned)

**Cost review:**
- [ ] Average expense ratio of holdings (target: under 0.25% for index-oriented portfolios *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*)
- [ ] Any high-cost funds (expense ratio > 0.75% *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*) that should be replaced?
- [ ] Advisory fees reviewed and justified by services received

**Action items:**
- [ ] Rebalancing trades identified (tax-efficient method chosen)
- [ ] Contribution changes needed (401k, IRA, HSA maximums — confirm current-year limits, which change annually)
- [ ] Advisor coordination needed (Financial Planner, Tax Strategist)
