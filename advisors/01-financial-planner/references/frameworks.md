# Financial Planning Frameworks

> are durable. Any year-specific dollar figure is labeled "illustrative — verify current-year value."
> Confirm the live number before relying on it.

## 1. Financial Health Snapshot

**When to use:** Opening any financial planning consultation — establishes the client's baseline.

**Net Worth = Total Assets − Total Liabilities.**
Assets: liquid (checking/savings/MM), tax-advantaged accounts, taxable investments, real estate
(market value), business equity, other. Liabilities: mortgages, student/auto loans, credit cards,
business debt with personal guarantee, other.

**Cash Flow = Total Monthly Income − Total Monthly Expenses.**
Savings Rate = Monthly Savings ÷ Monthly Gross Income.

**Benchmarks (durable ratios):**
- Emergency fund: 3–6 months of essential expenses (higher for variable income)
- Savings rate: 15–20% of gross income for retirement (including employer match)
- Debt-to-income: total monthly debt payments under ~36% of gross income

## 2. Financial Priority Stack

**When to use:** Client has limited cash flow and needs to know where to direct money first.

Fund each step before the next, unless a compelling reason to deviate:
1. **Starter emergency fund** — ~1 month of essential expenses.
2. **Employer 401(k) match** — contribute enough to capture the full match (an immediate return).
3. **High-interest debt payoff** — debt above ~7% (see Debt Payoff Framework).
4. **HSA** — if on a high-deductible plan; triple tax advantage. Limit ≈ $4,300 individual /
   $8,550 family *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*.
5. **Roth IRA** — annual max ≈ $7,000 (≈ $8,000 if 50+) *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*;
   income limits apply.
6. **401(k) to maximum** — employee deferral ≈ $23,500 (≈ $31,000 if 50+) *(illustrative — verify
   current-year value)*.
7. **Taxable brokerage** — tax-efficient funds (broad index funds/ETFs).
8. **Other goals** — education (529), real estate down payment, etc.

**Deviate when:** Roth income limits exceeded (backdoor Roth / traditional); business owner
(Solo 401(k)/SEP-IRA allow much higher contributions); very high-interest debt (prioritize payoff).

## 3. Retirement Projection Framework

**When to use:** "Am I on track?" or determining a target savings rate.

**Inputs:** current retirement savings; annual contribution; years to retirement; expected return
(use conservative real assumptions, e.g. 3–4% real); target annual retirement income (today's dollars).

**Calculation:**
```
FV of current savings    = Current Savings × (1 + r)^n
FV of contributions      = Annual Contribution × [((1 + r)^n − 1) / r]
Projected balance        = sum of the two
Sustainable withdrawal   = Projected balance × 0.04   (4% rule — a guideline, not a guarantee)
Income replacement rate  = Sustainable withdrawal ÷ current annual income
```

**Target:** Replace ~70–85% of pre-retirement income (durable guideline). Subtract estimated Social
Security from the income target; the portfolio covers the gap.

**Flags:** covers <60% of target → significant gap; covers 100%+ → on track.

## 4. Debt Payoff Framework

**When to use:** Client carries multiple debts and needs a payoff strategy.

**Avalanche (lowest total interest):** order debts by interest rate, highest first; minimums on all,
extra to the highest rate, then roll down. Best for the disciplined / high-rate debt.

**Snowball (most motivating):** order by balance, smallest first; minimums on all, extra to the
smallest, then roll up. Best when motivation is the constraint.

**Hybrid:** knock out one or two small balances for a quick win, then switch to avalanche.

Always pay at least the minimum on every debt before extra payments.

## 5. Tax-Efficiency Checklist

**When to use:** Reviewing investment and savings strategy for tax optimization.

**Asset location:**
- [ ] Tax-inefficient assets (bonds, REITs, high-dividend) → tax-advantaged accounts
- [ ] Tax-efficient assets (broad index funds, long-term growth) → taxable accounts
- [ ] International funds → consider taxable (foreign tax credit)

**Contribution sequencing:**
- [ ] Capturing the full employer 401(k) match?
- [ ] Roth IRA eligible? MAGI limits ≈ $161k single / $240k married *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*
- [ ] HSA maxed if on an HDHP?
- [ ] 401(k) at the maximum?

**Roth conversions:** favorable in lower-income/"gap" years, or to reduce future RMDs. Convert up to
the top of the current bracket; pay tax from non-IRA funds.

**Tax-loss harvesting:** offset gains with realized losses; avoid wash sales (30 days before/after).

**RMDs:** begin around age 73 *(illustrative — verify current-year value)*; consider Qualified
Charitable Distributions (QCDs) if charitably inclined.
